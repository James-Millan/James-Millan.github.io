During the covid-19 lockdowns of 2020-2021 I did barely any rigorous exercise. After falling ill with the virus in the middle of May 2021 I decided that it was time for a change. After recovering, I had fallen to about 70kg in weight which is quite low for my height. So my focus was on building strength and hence muscle.

At the start I tried learning as many different movements as possible. Before arriving at a routine that I do weekly that works for me. For the best results I believe it is optimal to perform the same movements over and over increasing the overall number: sets x reps x weight each week. Currently, below is the 4-day split I do each week. I run mesocycles in which each mesocycle is 3 weeks. Week 1 is strength week, 85% of 1rm for 3 reps and 120 seconds rest for all movements. Week 2 is hypertrophy week and is 75% of 1rm for 6 reps and 75 seconds rest for all movements. Week 3 is endurance week and is 65% of 1rm 12 reps. You then either test or repeat a cycle at a slightly higher hypothetical 1rm (usually +2.5kg).

Day 1: Squat Day

Movement Muscles worked Personal notes
Barbell Back Squat Glutes, Quads, Hamstrings Wide stance, feet rotated facing outwards slightly. Make sure to load the weight through the glutes, hamstrings and quads rather than through your knee, which is very bad!
Barbell Deficit Lunges Glutes, Quads Stand on a 25kg bumper plate, lunge backwards then return foot to plate but don't put weight fully on the returning leg. Do both legs before resting. There is a glute focus here created by the deficit plate.
Romainian Deadlifts (RDLs) Hamstrings, Glutes Hinge position. Keep the bar close to your legs and push your bum backwards.
Bulgarian Split Squats Quads, Glutes Remain upright for a quad focus (what I do) or lean forwards for more of a glute focus.
Leg Extensions Quads Quick on the way up, pause, slow on the way down making sure not the let the stack hit the bottom in order to maintain the tension throughout.

Day 2: Rest Day

Day 3: Bench press Day

Movement Muscles worked Personal notes
Barbell Bench Press Pecs, Shoulders, Triceps, Upper Back Arch back, engaging back muscles when setting up. Slow bar on the way down then power on the way up.
Dips Pecs, Shoulders, Triceps Make sure to lean forward when doing them - makes it harder and works muscles better.
Dumbbell bench press Pecs, Shoulders, Triceps Go down in like a triangle you should 'feel your pecs lengthening' then meet together at the top.
EZ bar french curl Triceps Put the bar further back towards your head than you would for a benchpress. Remember to keep your elbows narrow.
Incline Bench press (on smith machine) Pecs, Shoulders Wide elbows (unlike regular bench press) and don't fully extend elbows at the top.
DB standing shoulder press Shoulders, Triceps Two dumbbells resting on your shoulder, press them above your head.

Day 4: Rest Day

Day 5: Deadlift Day

Movement Muscles worked Personal notes
Deadlift Back, Glutes, Hamstrings Use wrist wraps, make sure to maintain the 'brace position' before starting to lift.
Glute Bridges Glutes, Hamstrings Use a 12inch box if below 6ft else use 18inch box
Lat pull down Lats Elevate scapula then pull down making sure to engage the lats. Pause at the bottom then slowly back up making sure not to hit the stack.
Bent over row Lats Perform the start of an RDL, then pull the bar halfway between your sternum and your belly button. Your arms should feel a bit like you're a T-rex. Pause at the top for optimality.
Stiff arm pulldown Lats, Rear delts, Triceps Maintain the same 'arm angle' throughout. Perform in a hinge position

Day 6: Overhead Press Day

Movement Muscles worked Personal notes
Overhead Press Anterior Deltoid, Medial Deltoid, Triceps, Trapezius Take bar in a front squat position and press overhead. Use strict form as to not bring in the lower back.
Arnold Press Posterior, Lateral, and Anterior deltoids, Trapezius, Triceps Push the dumbbells up like a corkscrew.
Lateral Raises Medial and anterior Deltoids, Trapezius Power on the way up. Hold for a few seconds at the top. Slow on the way down.
Upright Row Trapezius, Medial and Anterior Deltoids, Rhomboids Hold the bar and move your elbows as high up as you can. Retraction and protraction of scapula at the start and end of each rep.
Overhead Tricep extension Triceps Push the cables vertically above your head ensuring the elbows do not flare out.

Nutrition

Your Basal Metabolic Rate is the number of calories you burn each day just by existing. Calculate this here. In order to gain muscle: Try and be in a 200-300 caloric surplus each day. This includes your BMR + any amount of calories that you burn of exercising in the day. A good rule of thumb is "you shouldn't feel hungry during the day when trying to gain muscle". Aim to eat high protein meals. This is optimal for muscle recovery, and protein is the best at satiating you because when you eat protein, the hormone Leptin is released which inhibits hunger. Similarly, you burn 20-30% of calories in protein through digestion. It is for this reason that a high protein diet is also great if you are trying to lose weight. As well as strength training 3 times a week, try and hit 10,000 steps every day. If you can maintain a 500 calorie deficit, then that's 3500 calories lost per week. This equates to losing 1 pound of body fat per week.
